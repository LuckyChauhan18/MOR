## Historical Context of US-Azerbaijan Relations

The diplomatic relationship between the United States and Azerbaijan has evolved significantly since Azerbaijan gained independence from the Soviet Union in 1991. Initially, the U.S. recognized Azerbaijan's sovereignty and established formal diplomatic relations in 1992. This marked the beginning of a partnership that has been shaped by various geopolitical interests, particularly in energy security and regional stability.

Over the years, several key agreements have been forged between the two nations. Notably, the 1994 "Contract of the Century" allowed Western companies to invest in Azerbaijan's oil sector, which has been crucial for both the Azerbaijani economy and U.S. energy interests. Additionally, the U.S. has supported Azerbaijan's integration into international organizations, further solidifying their partnership.

However, the relationship has not been without challenges. Regional conflicts, particularly the long-standing tensions between Azerbaijan and Armenia over Nagorno-Karabakh, have influenced U.S. foreign policy in the region. The U.S. has often found itself balancing its support for Azerbaijan with the need to promote peace and stability in the South Caucasus.
